# Game Design: Daybreak Protocol

## One-Sentence Pitch

Decode six June archive transmissions with Turing-inspired algorithms before daylight runs out, while every choice physically moves the solstice sun.

## Design Pillars

1. **Reasoning changes time.** Correct answers restore daylight; wrong assumptions accelerate sunset.
2. **Algorithms are the action.** Caesar shifts, XOR, compression, parity, contradiction, and binary search are the mechanics rather than decoration.
3. **A turning point, not a finish line.** The final ending depends on remaining light, reinforcing the solstice as a transition.
4. **Learn through play.** Every answer reveals a concise explanation of why the algorithm works.

## Core Loop

1. Read a short archive transmission.
2. Inspect an encoded signal and choose one of four decoding leads.
3. Spend daylight on a hint or risk a wrong answer.
4. Restore a Bombe relay and receive an algorithm explanation.
5. Reach one of three victory endings, or let the horizon go dark.

## Scoring

- Base puzzle reward increases by chapter.
- Remaining daylight adds a speed bonus.
- Consecutive correct first attempts build a streak bonus.
- Correct answers restore eight seconds.
- Wrong answers cost sixteen seconds.
- Hints cost ten seconds and reset the streak.

## Audience And Session

The game is designed for players with no cryptography background. A first run lasts approximately three to five minutes, making it easy for judges to play and easy to demonstrate in a short video.
